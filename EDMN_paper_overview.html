EDMN: Enhanced Distribution Matching with Quantification-Aware Neural Networks for Multiclass Quantification
Danuka Malinda · Dhanushka Senarathna · Spyros Tragoudas
Southern Illinois University Carbondale, USA
Machine learning Quantification Neural networks Distribution matching 58 benchmark datasets

Quantification is the task of estimating the class distribution in an unlabeled dataset. Existing multiclass methods rely on traditional classifiers, failing to capture inter-class relationships. EDMN trains a neural network with a quantification-based loss to generate probability outputs suitable for distribution matching, then estimates class distribution using inter-class relationships via an enhanced matching approach.

§2
EDM method
Inter-class conditional histograms from training; neighborhood search + distribution matching for estimation. 14 dissimilarity functions evaluated.
§3
EANN framework
Two conditions C1 (sufficient entropy) and C2 (class identity preserved). Composite loss LDM + LCE with label smoothing and temperature scaling (τ=1.5, ε=0.025).
§3 — theory
Formal analysis
Entropy bounds (Properties 1–2), KL decomposition (Property 3), mutual information perspective. Proves C1 and C2 simultaneously satisfied.
§4
Experiments
Ablation study across EDM, EDM+LS, EDM+τ, EDMN. Convergence in ~11 iterations. Hyperparameter sensitivity: b=90 bins, τ=1.5.
